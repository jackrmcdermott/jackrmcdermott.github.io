Chegg Skills — Today

Today, I lead a growth team within Chegg Skills, a career-focused division within this leading edtech company. Our team drives the growth of online skills courses designed for working adults.

Over two years, we built new courses in high-growth fields like Cybersecurity & Data Analytics, and introduced them to learners in new markets, including the education as a benefit space.

At Chegg, I also led customer research and business design for new products for college students. This early research contributed to product directions now under wide-scale development. I'll share more on that sometime soon.

Panorama Education — 2014-2019

I began my career at Panorama Education. I was the company's first marketing hire, and I helped launch new products that improved student outcomes in K-12 school districts. While there, I moved to San Francisco to open our next office.

Panorama led the way in measuring the growth of the "whole child." I produced documentary shorts and wrote data stories about social-emotional learning in EdSurge & Education Week. After five years, Panorama had grown from 8 to 140 employees and served more than 10 million students.

Get to Know You Survey — 2015

While at Panorama, I led a team of engineers and educators to build the 'Get to Know You' Survey. 100,000+ students and teachers used the survey to learn what they had in common with each other. We collaborated with Harvard researchers and their findings were featured on NPR's Hidden Brain podcast.

Speech4Good — 2011-2013

I founded Speech4Good while in college. Our mission was to create mobile apps for people (like me) with speech disorders. I led product and marketing for apps that reached 25,000+ users. We raised some funding and were named one of Inc. Magazine's "Coolest College Startups."

Fluently — 2012

While building speech apps, I launched an app project called Fluently. The app detected moments of stuttering and provided real-time speech analysis. The technical work became more difficult than imagined, so we sunsetted the project (still one of my favorites) a year later.
