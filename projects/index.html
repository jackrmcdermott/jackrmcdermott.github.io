In 2014, I joined Panorama Education as the first hire in marketing as the YC-backed company launched new survey measures — on topics like student engagement, school safety, parent satisfaction — for more than 75 public school districtsto. I was a Marketing Manager and implemented our initial growth and acquisition campaigns.

While at Panorama, I led a team of engineers, designers, and educators in building the 'Get to Know You' Survey — a free online survey tool that helps students and teachers explore their shared interests and similarities. We based the survey tool on the latest research on teacher-student relationships and, soon enough, more than 100k students found something in common with their teacher. We also worked closely with a research team from Harvard to measure efficacy — their work would later be featured on NPR's Hidden Brain podcast.

Prior to that, I co-founded a startup called Speech4Good, which developed mobile applications for people (like me) with speech impediments.

As a part of Speech4Good, I launched a project called Fluently that attempted to measure and detect specific moments of disfluent speech like stuttering. Our team developed an iOS app that speech therapists used to more accurately support their students. This work turned out to be more technically difficult than we had hoped, so we sunsetted the project (still one of my favorite) about a year later.

More recently, I've put my degree in political science to use designing and managing the development of digital + political campaign tools for progressive candidates. These tools have connected candidates with voters far more efficiently and have shown support for key groups like our veterans.
